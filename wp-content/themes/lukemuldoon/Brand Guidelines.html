01 · Logo
01 · THE LOGO

The primary mark.

PG. 03

A coal-black tile with the bottom-right corner clipped, containing chartreuse lm lowercase letters set in Space Grotesk 700. The snipped corner is the brand's signature — a small piece of craft missing, by design. The square structure echoes a creative-tool product icon; the snip says "hand-finished".

CONSTRUCTION
Built on a 320pt grid. The corner snip removes a 56pt isosceles triangle from the bottom-right (≈17.5% of the side length).
CLEAR SPACE
Reserve a margin equal to the cap-height of the "l" on all sides. Nothing else lives in that zone — no type, no edges.
MINIMUM SIZE
24×24 px on screen. 8×8 mm in print. Below this, the corner snip stops reading.

02 · VARIANTS

Three locked forms.

PG. 04

The mark works in three colour forms. Choose by background, not by mood. The primary form is the default; the others exist to solve specific contrast problems.

Primary Coal · Lime
Lime field Primary on Lime
Inverse Lime · Coal
Monochrome Coal · Chalk
Lockup with the wordmark. When pairing the tile with the full name, set "Luke Muldoon" in Space Grotesk 600 at the same cap-height as the tile, with a gap equal to one-half of that cap-height. For nav bars, use the domain wordmark lukemuldoon.co.uk in JetBrains Mono instead.

03 · DON'TS

What ruins it.

PG. 05

Six ways to destroy a perfectly good logo. The mark is small and precise — every choice in its construction is doing a job. If you change one, the rest stop working.

01 · Don't
Don't skew, rotate, or stretch the letters. They're set on a specific optical axis.
02 · Don't
Don't soften the corner snip into a rounded radius. The snip is the brand — keep it sharp.
03 · Don't
Don't introduce gradients, drop shadows, or any "effect". The mark is flat. Always.
04 · Don't
Don't change the typeface, weight, case, or letterforms. Lowercase Space Grotesk 700, no exceptions.
05 · Don't
Don't recolour to anything outside the approved palette. Only Coal, Chalk, or Lime are allowed.
06 · Don't
Don't shrink the letters inside the tile. They should fill it — that's why it feels like a button you can press.

07 · Voice
07 · VOICE & TONE

Punchy, terse, plain-English.

PG. 09

Write like you'd talk to a smart small-business owner who's been burned once. Outcomes first, technical details only when they make the outcome believable.

01
Short.
Sentences under 15 words. Bullets under 8.
02
Specific.
Numbers, not adjectives. "0.6s LCP", not "blazing fast".
03
First-person.
"I build" not "we deliver". One person, not an agency.
04
Outcome-led.
Lead with what they get. The how comes second.

✓ Do

Don't lose customers to slow pages.
Hand-coded. No page builders, no template debt.
Scan a competitor. See how you compare.

✗ Don't

"We empower brands to deliver world-class digital experiences."
"Our cutting-edge stack leverages best-in-class tooling."
"Let's hop on a quick discovery call to align on your needs."
"Pixel-perfect, mobile-first, future-proof solutions."
